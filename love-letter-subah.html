From Aahnaf
My dearest Gutul,

I have been trying to write this for longer than I care to admit. Every time I start, the words come out smaller than what I actually mean. So let me try plainly: after three years, you remain the one thing I am most certain of. Not in a passive way — in the way that matters, the kind of certainty that has been tested and has held.

I have watched you navigate three years of life with a quiet grace that I find genuinely remarkable. You carry your kindness without announcing it. You absorb the difficult moments — including mine — with a composure that I have never fully managed to earn. When I lose my footing, you do not make a production of steadying things. You simply stay. That is not a small thing, Gutul. That is the whole thing.

I notice you, constantly. The way your hair tells me your mood before you say a word. The way you overthink in circles and always somehow arrive at the most generous possible conclusion. The way you blush — still, after all this time — as if affection is something that surprises you. It does not surprise me. I am the one who never runs out of it where you are concerned.

You are brilliant, patient, and far more tender than you let most people see. You love white roses and extreme amounts of sugar in your coffee. You are a committed foodie with a stomach that occasionally disagrees with your ambitions. You are a devoted daughter, a diligent student, and a woman of real faith and quiet conviction. You are, without reservation, the most interesting person in any room I share with you.

Three years. And I would choose this, again, without hesitation.

Yours, completely and without condition — Aahnaf

On knowing you

The Hair Tells Me Everything

Texture What it means What I do
Straight She is content. The day is good. Happy Gutul Nothing. Just watch. It is a good day.
Wavy There is friction somewhere. She is holding something. Annoyed Gutul Ask gently. Or wait. Both work, depending on the day.
Curly She is deep in thought, or hurting quietly. Reflective Gutul Sit with her. No words required.

Three years of careful observation. I have earned this knowledge.

What I carry

The things I love about you

01

The blush, still

Three years in and a single sincere compliment still colours your face. I find this extraordinary. I will never stop trying.

02

Your particular kind of calm

You do not perform steadiness. When things are difficult, you simply become quieter and more present. I have learned more from that than from anything else.

03

How seriously you take things

Your academics. Your faith. Your friendships. You do not do anything halfway. That is a rare quality and I admire it deeply.

04

Your love for white

White roses. White dresses. White kurtis. It is the most honest colour and it suits you perfectly.

05

The way you overthink

You spiral, yes — but you always arrive somewhere thoughtful and generous. The process is yours. The destination is always good.

06

The version of you very few people see

Late, guard down, talking freely — entirely yourself. That is the version I feel most fortunate to know.

07

Your kindness, given without announcement

You give quietly. You remember things. You show up. Most people do not notice how much of this you do. I notice.

08

All of it, honestly

Every version. Every mood the hair announces. Every impossible coffee order. Every quiet, steady moment. All of it.

Things I have catalogued

Quirks I have grown fond of

A foodie with principles and a complicated stomach

Momos, pizza, pasta, kacchi — ordered with full conviction every time. The consequences are always secondary to the experience. I respect it.

Coffee, but unreasonably sweet

The sugar level is non-negotiable. This is a position she holds with complete seriousness. I stopped questioning it around year one.

She reads between every line

A single off-tone message and she already knows something is wrong. She knows me, sometimes, before I do. That is both unsettling and the greatest comfort I have.

Private about affection, precise about care

She does not perform warmth publicly. But in private, she is attentive in ways that are specific to you, deliberate and real. That is the better kind.

Her scent is unreasonably comforting

Something warm and sweet — butterscotch and something softer beneath it. I cannot explain it further. It simply smells like home.

What I intend

Promises I make to Gutul

I will keep showing up — especially on the days it requires effort.
I will learn to be steadier, because you deserve that from me.
I will never stop noticing the small things. I have always been paying attention.
I will celebrate what you achieve, even when you forget to.
I will remember the coffee order. Always extreme sugar. No compromises.
I will love every version of you — straight, wavy, curly — without condition.
